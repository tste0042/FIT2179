Where Australia Runs

Participation, access, and outdoor running conditions across Australia.

Running is one of Australia's most accessible physical activities, but participation, organised running access, and outdoor conditions vary across the country.

01

How popular is running?

AusPlay estimates that 3.74 million Australian adults participated in running or jogging in 2025. The first section places running against other common activities, then shows how participation differs by age and gender.

02

Where do Australians run?

Participation varies by state and territory, and parkrun locations show where organised weekly 5km running opportunities are physically located.

Running participation by state and territory

Darker states and territories show higher adult running/jogging participation rates.

Parkrun locations are clustered around population centres

Each point represents one standard Australian 5km parkrun event.

Queensland has the most parkrun events

Raw event counts favour larger states, so the next section adjusts by population.

03

Is organised running equally accessible?

Counting events alone can be misleading. Normalising by population gives a clearer comparison of organised running access.

Tasmania has the highest event access per person

Smaller populations can produce higher access ratios even with fewer total events.

Participation and access do not perfectly align

The scatterplot compares AusPlay running participation with parkrun events per 100,000 people.

04

Where and when are conditions best for running?

Outdoor running is shaped by seasonal temperature. This section compares average monthly maximum temperatures across capital cities.

Summer heat changes the running experience

Darwin and Brisbane stay warmer across the year, while Hobart, Melbourne and Canberra have more cool or comfortable months.

Comfortable running months by capital city

A derived summary translates monthly temperature into a simpler running-comfort comparison.

Final takeaway

Running is a major Australian physical activity, but the data shows that participation, organised access, and outdoor conditions are unevenly distributed. Younger adults participate at higher rates, parkrun access is not always strongest in the largest states, and climate changes the running experience across cities and seasons.

Sources and notes

Data sources: AusPlay participation data from the Australian Sports Commission AusPlay results; parkrun event location data from parkrun Australia events; population data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics National, state and territory population release; climate data from the Bureau of Meteorology Climate of Australia major cities data; and Australian state boundary geometry from rowanhogan/australian-states GeoJSON.

Data processing: Cleaned CSV files were created from the source datasets for use in Vega-Lite. Derived fields include parkrun events per 100,000 people, state-level access rankings, participation-versus-access comparisons, and simplified running-temperature categories based on monthly average maximum temperatures.
